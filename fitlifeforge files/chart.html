BMI Calculator

 Underweight (BMI less than 18.5):

- Aim to increase calorie intake with nutrient-dense foods.
- Include healthy fats like avocados, nuts, seeds, and olive oil.
- Consume protein-rich foods such as lean meats, fish, poultry, eggs, legumes, and dairy products.
- Incorporate complex carbohydrates from whole grains, fruits, and vegetables.
- Eat frequent, smaller meals and snacks throughout the day.
- Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water.


 Normal Weight (BMI 18.5 - 24.9):

- Maintain a balanced diet consisting of a variety of foods from all food groups.
- Focus on whole, minimally processed foods.
- Include lean proteins, such as fish, poultry, tofu, legumes, and dairy products.
- Incorporate a variety of colorful fruits and vegetables to ensure a wide range of nutrients.
- Choose whole grains like quinoa, brown rice, oats, and whole wheat bread.
- Monitor portion sizes to prevent overeating.


  Overweight (BMI 25 - 29.9):

- Aim for a slight calorie deficit to promote gradual weight loss.
- Emphasize whole foods over processed foods.
- Increase intake of vegetables, which are low in calories and high in nutrients and fiber.
- Choose lean protein sources to support muscle mass while reducing overall calorie intake.
- Limit added sugars and refined carbohydrates.
- Be mindful of portion sizes and practice mindful eating.


Obese (BMI 30 or greater):

- Work with a registered dietitian or healthcare professional to develop a personalized plan.
- Focus on whole, nutrient-dense foods to promote satiety and provide essential nutrients.
- Incorporate regular physical activity along with dietary changes for weight management.
- Include lean protein sources, fiber-rich fruits and vegetables, whole grains, and healthy fats.
- Monitor portion sizes and practice mindful eating to prevent overconsumption.
- Limit intake of high-calorie, low-nutrient foods and beverages such as sugary drinks, fried foods, and desserts.
